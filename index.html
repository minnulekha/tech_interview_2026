OUR VISION

What is FOSS?

Free and open-source software (FOSS) is software that is available for anyone to use, modify, and share without restrictions. "Free" refers to freedom, not necessarily price—meaning users have the liberty to access the source code and adapt it. This approach fosters transparency, community-driven development, and the creation of software that benefits everyone.

Our Objectives

01

Empower students to contribute to the global FOSS community.

02

Host workshops and collaborative coding sessions.

03

Advocate for open-source adoption at the university level.

04

Facilitate student participation to enhance career prospects.

FOSS vs Proprietary

  • Transparency: Open source allows for auditing; proprietary code is hidden.
  • Flexibility: Modify software to specific needs vs limited vendor features.
  • Collaboration: Global community innovation vs single-company control.
  • Cost: Typically free to use, avoiding heavy licensing fees.
  • Independence: Avoid vendor lock-in and control your own software longevity.

FOSS for the People

FOSS empowers individuals, schools, and small businesses to embrace technology without high costs, fostering digital literacy and equal opportunities for all.
